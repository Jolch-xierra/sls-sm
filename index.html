Page 3 – Meaning & Jobs/Skills (Think + Feel)

Page 3 – Why Do People Work in Sport?

People in sport help others grow, feel confident, and work as a team. A good coach demonstrates care, respect, and perseverance daily in their training sessions.

Short Story: Coach A

Coach A trains the school soccer team. When players make mistakes, Coach A doesn't get angry. Instead, Coach A stops play, explains with care, and encourages them to try again. When a player got injured during a match, Coach A immediately rushed to check on them and guided them safely to the first aid station.

Q4. What matters MOST to a good coach like Coach A?

People in sport use social and emotional skills, communication, and collaboration – important 21st Century Competencies.

Secondary – Sports Careers and the Sports Ecosystem

Exploring behind-the-scenes roles, the sports ecosystem, and how my values and strengths connect to meaningful work.

Page 1 – Introduction & Hook (See)

Page 1 – Exploring Careers in the Sports Industry

Sport is about much more than what happens on the field of play. To support athletic excellence and community health, we rely on a sophisticated industry behind the scenes.

In this lesson, you will:

  • Uncover diverse back-room sporting careers in Singapore.
  • Analyse how school subjects connect directly to practical career options.
  • Examine the critical competencies required to succeed in tomorrow's workplace.

You will practise: Critical & Inventive Thinking, and Communication & Collaboration.

My Learning Goals:

Sports Careers Roles Highlights:

📊 Data Analyst

"Someone gets paid to watch every game." Tracks metrics, biomechanical outputs, and helps team-coaches build strategies.

🎟️ Event Operations Executive

"The roar of the crowd doesn’t happen by accident." Coordinates logistics, security, ticketing, and scheduling for local tournaments.

👟 Equipment Designer

"The kit your favourite athlete wears was designed by an engineer." Applies physics and material science to build lighter, safer sports gear.

📸 Photographer

"Every moment is captured." Tells compelling athlete stories and administers communications on web-platforms.

Pre-University – Designing My Sports-Related Pathway

Connecting my values and strengths to sports clusters, future roles, and concrete post-secondary pathways.

Page 1 – Introduction & Hook (See)

Page 1 – Designing My Sports-Related Pathway

Pre-University is a critical junction. Navigating the athletic landscape at an advanced level means mapping professional accreditation boards, industry pillars, and advanced tertiary programs.

In this lesson, you will draw heavily upon your Critical & Inventive Thinking, Information & Communication Skills, and Civic Literacy.

Pre-U Learning Goals Checklist:

The Five Core Sports Clusters in Singapore:

1. High-Performance & Sports Science

Biomechanics, physiology, video analysis, coaching pedagogy.

2. Sports Medicine & Rehabilitation

Physiotherapy, clinical orthopaedics, occupational therapy.

3. Sports Business, Management & Events

Sponsorship sales, facilities operations, club admin.

4. Sports Media, Communications & Technology

Journalism, broadcasting, wearable tech development.

5. Education, Coaching & Community Development

ActiveSG managers, PE teachers, fitness instructors.

Q1. A Sports Biomechanist uses force plates and 3D cameras to help high-jump athletes refine their takeoff angles. Which cluster is this?
